Welcome

We're so honored to celebrate with you, and your presence truly means the world to us. We completely understand if this weekend doesn't work out for any reason, but we're so grateful for your love and support either way.

Our wedding will take place on a beautiful private property in Snohomish with a relaxed, outdoor, campout-style atmosphere and just a touch of elegance. Our hope is to create a weekend that's welcoming, memorable, and filled with time to simply enjoy being together.

Everyone is invited to join us for a rehearsal dinner and welcome hang on Friday, July 31st. On Saturday, August 1st, we'll provide appetizers, snacks, drinks, and dinner following the ceremony.

For the rest of the weekend, please plan to bring simple breakfast and lunch options. If you'd rather not pack food, there are several grocery stores and restaurants just a short drive away.

Once the reception winds down on Saturday evening, we'll keep the celebration going with campfire festivities, DJ sets, and fire dancing. During the day, you'll find lawn games, music, cozy creative nooks, and plenty of space to relax, explore the property, and spend time with friends and family.

Whether you're joining us for the whole weekend or just the wedding day, we can't wait to celebrate with you!

Our Story

Our story began over ten years ago at our friends Jon & Christal Fitzgerald's wedding in San Diego. 🙌

At the time, Chelsey was living in San Diego, and Michael had been there for about ten years before getting ready to move back home to Washington. Somewhere between the wedding festivities and geeking out over our shared love of electronic music, we exchanged contact information and became creative collaborators, with Michael making beats that Chelsey would eventually sing over... even if it was a year later. Michael was patient and didn't seem to mind. 😂

Over the next ten years, our friendship, despite being across state lines, grew into something much deeper than artistic collaboration. We shared music, life, fears, laughter, and everything in between, always somehow staying connected no matter where life took us.

In October 2025, while Chelsey was living in Denver, Colorado, and Michael was still in Washington, we decided it was finally time to go on our first date. Michael flew out, and together we went to our very first Red Rocks show to see REZZ. It was a pretty great first date.

From there, we kept "dating" over the interwebs (Michael found a pretty bad ass app that allowed us to watch shows/movies with perfect timing so there wouldn't be a lag...yeah, it's Chelsey writing this - I think he's pretty cool), visiting each other whenever we could, until we eventually decided to take a chance on love... and maybe save a few dollars on plane tickets. 😉

Just a few months into dating, Michael flew to Denver, and we packed up Chelsey's magical (and very underrated) Prius for the drive to Washington. We were fully prepared for a winter blizzard—with snow tires, emergency blankets, and all—but somehow enjoyed beautiful weather through the Rockies. Naturally, Washington welcomed us with the thickest fog imaginable for the last 45 minutes of the drive.

When we finally made it through, we arrived at a beautiful home and a new chapter that Chelsey knew she was ready for.

About six months into dating, after countless late nights laughing until our faces hurt and showing up for each other the same way we always had, we couldn't help but think, "This is my person." And, as they say... "When you know, you know."

We know it might seem like things moved quickly... but if you really think about it, we took it pretty slow. 😉

Airport Access

The closest major airport is Seattle-Tacoma International Airport (Sea-Tac), about 45–60 minutes away depending on traffic.

Parking & Arrival

Parking will be available on-site. The property is mostly grass and dirt, so please plan accordingly:

  • Low-clearance cars: the ground is fairly flat, just take it slow
  • Heels: we highly recommend skipping them or bringing a backup pair 😄

Carpooling with friends is encouraged! We'll have signage and/or people guiding you when you arrive.

Food & Meals

What We're Providing

  • July 31: Rehearsal Dinner & Welcome Hang — everyone is invited! We'll just need a general headcount ahead of time, so if you haven't already RSVP'd with this information, we'd greatly appreciate it. We'll provide dinner and drinks for the evening.
  • August 1: Appetizers, snacks, drinks, and dinner following the ceremony.

We'll also be sending out a short questionnaire before the wedding to gather food preferences for both dinners.

Breakfast & Lunch

For breakfasts and lunches, please plan accordingly. Bringing simple, easy options such as yogurt, fruit, sandwiches, or your favorite snacks is a great idea.

While there won't be access to the kitchen inside the house, we'll do our best to store perishable items for you. If you'll need cooler space, please let us know ahead of time so we can plan accordingly. Camping stoves are also welcome if you'd like to prepare your own meals.

If you'd rather not pack food, there are several grocery stores and restaurants just a short drive away.

BYO Welcome

We'll have drinks available during the rehearsal dinner, cocktail hour, and reception. Outside of those times, feel free to BYO your favorite beverages or campfire treats to enjoy throughout the weekend! Just remember to drink responsibly and help us keep the property beautiful.

Camping & Accommodations

We'd love for as many guests as possible to stay on-site and enjoy the full weekend together! Camping is welcome and encouraged.

Feel free to bring a tent, sleeping bag, bedding, and anything else that will make your stay comfortable.

We've received many of your camping gear requests already — thank you! If you haven't let us know yet and will need to borrow any camping equipment, please reach out as soon as you can so we can do our best to make arrangements.

Our gracious host has generously opened his beautiful property for our wedding weekend, and we'd love to help care for his home in return. To respect his family's privacy and preserve the space, the house itself will generally not be open for guest use. The indoor restroom will remain available primarily for guests 65+ and families with young children who may need easier restroom access or a place to take a short break. Thank you so much for helping us care for this special place.

Think of it as "camping-lite" — comfortable, cozy, and outdoorsy.

Accommodations Note

If camping isn't your style, there are several nearby hotels and Airbnbs. Since Airbnb options in the area can be somewhat limited, we recommend booking a local hotel sooner rather than later for the best availability, comfort, and value.

Whether you're camping under the stars or staying nearby, we're simply excited to spend the weekend celebrating with you.

Families & Little Adventurers

We're so excited to celebrate with all of our little adventurers! Throughout the day — and well into the evening — kids are welcome to enjoy the festivities right alongside everyone else. We know many little ones will especially love the music, campfires, and fire dancing!

For families staying overnight, we'll also have a Family/Kids' Camp area available for anyone who'd appreciate a quieter place to settle in once bedtime rolls around. It's completely optional, but we hope it makes the weekend a little more comfortable for families with young children.

During the day, we'll have lawn games, frisbees, and plenty of room to explore. If you'd like to help co-create the fun, feel free to bring toys, outdoor games, crafts, bubbles, or other kid-friendly activities to share — we'd love it!

Attire

We'd love for everyone to dress semi-formal for the ceremony! If you'd like to wear red or wine-colored accents, we'd love that! Those colors are part of our wedding palette, but there's absolutely no expectation — wear whatever makes you feel your best.

The ceremony and reception will be outdoors, so we highly recommend comfortable shoes. The property is well-maintained, but you'll still be walking on grass, gravel, and uneven ground in some areas.

As the evening settles in and the campfire gets going, don't be surprised if people start trading dress shoes for boots and cozy clothes. Feel free to bring a change of clothes if you'd like to get comfy for the night's festivities — or stay dressed to the nines! Either way, we're just excited to celebrate with you.

And, after all... it is Washington! While we're hoping for sunshine, we'll have plenty of easy-ups and covered areas throughout the property just in case Mother Nature decides to join the celebration. We also recommend bringing a jacket or sweater, as evenings can cool down once the sun sets.
