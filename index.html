Welcome

We're so honored to celebrate with you, and your presence truly means the world to us. We completely understand if this weekend doesn't work for you for any reason, but we're so grateful for your love and support either way 💛

Our wedding will take place on a beautiful private property in Snohomish — think a relaxed, outdoor, campout-style vibe with an elegant touch. We'll be providing a rehearsal dinner/welcome hang on July 31st (all are invited), along with hors d'oeuvres, drinks, and dinner on August 1st.

Driving Notes

  • About 10–15 minutes from downtown Snohomish
  • About 45–60 minutes from Seattle (depending on traffic)
  • Parking will be available on-site

Airport Access

The closest major airport is Seattle-Tacoma International Airport (Sea-Tac), about 45–60 minutes away depending on traffic. You'll likely want to rent a car for the weekend.

Parking & Arrival

Parking will be available on-site. The property is mostly grass and dirt, so please plan accordingly:

  • Low-clearance cars: the ground is fairly flat, just take it slow
  • Heels: we highly recommend skipping them or bringing a backup pair 😄

Carpooling with friends is encouraged! We'll have signage and/or people guiding you when you arrive.

Food & Meals

What We're Providing

  • July 31: Rehearsal dinner / welcome hang — everyone is invited! We'll just need a general headcount ahead of time.
  • August 1: Hors d'oeuvres and drinks after the ceremony, followed by dinner.

We'll be sending out a questionnaire ahead of time to gather food preferences for both the rehearsal dinner and wedding dinner.

Other Meals

For other meals, please plan accordingly — bringing easy breakfast items, lunches, or your favorite snacks is a great idea.

An indoor kitchen will be available for light use (breakfast/lunch-style meals) on August 1st and the morning of August 2nd. Let us know if you'd like access to a cooler for perishable items. Camping stoves are also welcome.

There are also nearby grocery stores and restaurants if you'd prefer to grab something in town.

Camping & Comfort

We'd love for guests to stay on-site and enjoy the full weekend together! Camping is welcome and encouraged.

You're welcome to bring a tent, bedding, and anything that will make you feel comfortable for the night.

If you're traveling from far away or don't have camping gear, we may be able to provide some — just let us know! We'll include questions about this in the RSVP/questionnaire.

There will be access to some covered areas and limited indoor space — please be mindful and respectful of the home.

This will be a "camping-lite" experience: comfortable and cozy, but still outdoorsy ✨

If camping isn't your thing, there are also nearby hotels and Airbnbs for additional comfort and privacy.

Accommodations Note

We are able to provide camping gear for those traveling from afar. Airbnb's are a bit limited at this time and we might actually recommend local hotels for a more comfortable and affordable stay. We recommend booking soon!

Attire

Think "dressy (semi-formal), but comfortable." This is a great opportunity to dress up and look sharp — but please keep in mind the outdoor setting.

The event will be on grass, so we recommend comfortable, ground-friendly shoes. Feel free to express your style — whether that leans more elevated or more relaxed. The goal is to look good and feel good.

And yes — red is totally acceptable! 😊

Cell service is generally available. We'll have power outlets on-site for charging. WiFi may be available but could be limited — plan accordingly.

🌤️ Weather

Early August in Snohomish is usually warm during the day and cooler in the evening. Bring layers!

What to Bring

  • A jacket, hoodie, or blanket for the evening
  • Comfortable shoes (grass-friendly is best!)
  • Camping gear if you're staying overnight
  • A reusable water bottle
  • Anything that helps you feel cozy and at home
